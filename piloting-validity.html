Promises & Pitfalls of Piloting

What pilot studies can (and cannot) do for validity in psychological research

▲ Pilots strengthen design decisions before data collection
▼ Misused pilots can undermine the credibility they aim to build
Construct Validity
Do measures & manipulations capture what they claim?
Pilots can
Think-aloud & cognitive interviews Check item interpretation Estimate reliability Validate & calibrate manipulations Test translations & cultural fit Detect demand cues
Watch out for
◆ Over-tuning measures to produce "clean" effects can narrow the construct
◆ Small-sample reliability estimates are unstable — don't prune items on them
◆ "Manipulation hacking" — iterating without documenting failed variants
Internal Validity
Can effects be attributed to the intended cause?
Pilots can
Verify randomisation pipeline Check baseline balance Estimate dropout & attrition Probe participant blinding Detect order & carryover effects Compare treatment vs control
Watch out for
◆ No problems in a small pilot ≠ no problems in the main study
◆ Selecting the variant with the largest effect shifts threats, doesn't remove them
◆ Pilot conditions may differ from the main study — assurances may not transfer
External Validity
Do findings generalise across people, settings & stimuli?
Pilots can
Compare recruitment channels Describe sample demographics Calibrate stimulus difficulty Test cross-device robustness Check cultural & contextual fit Refine eligibility criteria
Watch out for
◆ Convenience pilot samples ≠ target population — don't assume generalisability
◆ "Stimulus hacking" — keeping only stimuli with large effects, unreported
◆ Selecting the context that yields the largest pilot effect biases the main study
Statistical-Conclusion Validity
Are the analyses appropriate given design & data?
Pilots can
Estimate variance & ICCs Inform realistic power analyses Set exclusion thresholds Check model assumptions Identify appropriate model families Clarify confirmatory vs exploratory
Watch out for
◆ Small-pilot effect sizes are noisy — using them directly leads to underpowered studies
◆ Hidden model selection: trying many specs, reporting only the "best"
◆ Merging pilot & main data without adjustment inflates false-positive rates

Pilot studies are methodological due diligence — not a consequence-free sandbox. Transparent reporting of what was piloted, what changed, and why is essential to preserve the credibility that piloting aims to build.
